Shocking Facts About Coral Reefs

Coral Reefs Cover Less Than 1%
But they support 25% of all marine life.
Coral reefs occupy less than 1% of Earth's oceans, yet they are vital for maintaining biodiversity, acting as nurseries and safe havens for thousands of marine species. Their unique structure also helps to buffer coastlines against storms and erosion.
Half of Coral Reefs Are Gone
50% lost in the past 30 years.
Over the last three decades, pollution, unsustainable fishing practices, climate change, and coral bleaching events have destroyed half of the worldâ€™s reefs. Urgent action is needed to halt further loss.
1 Billion Depend on Reefs
Food, income and protection.
Coral reefs support fishing, tourism, and defense against storms for a billion people globally. Many communities rely on reefs for their livelihood and daily sustenance.
